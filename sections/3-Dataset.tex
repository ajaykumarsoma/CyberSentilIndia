\section{Dataset}

\subsection{Data Collection and Characteristics}
The dataset comprises 25,000 annotated samples collected from official Indian government
portals (\texttt{cybercrime.gov.in}, CERT-In, I4C) between January 2022 and December 2024.
It spans English (30\%), Hindi (25\%), Telugu (18\%), Tamil (15\%), and Malayalam (12\%),
with 45\% including images/screenshots (avg. 38 words/sample). Statistics are in
Table~\ref{tab:dataset}.

\subsection{Annotation and Ethics}
Annotation was performed by 15 trained experts (cybersecurity professionals and linguists)
with dual labeling and senior arbitration. Inter-annotator agreement: Fleiss' $\kappa = 0.86$
(overall), Cohen's $\kappa = 0.80$--$0.88$ (per language). All PII was anonymized; the
protocol was ethics-approved.

\subsection{Class Distribution}
The dataset spans 50 categories across 20 primary classes: financial fraud (35\%),
communication crimes (28\%), identity crimes (18\%), system attacks (12\%), and emerging
threats (7\%). Weighted loss mitigates class imbalance.

\subsection{Significance}
This is the first large-scale multilingual multimodal cybercrime dataset for India,
providing a benchmark for cross-lingual detection research.
